Los Angeles in July 2026: Sun, Sports & Star-Studded Events

July 1, 2026  ·  5 min read

← Back to Blog

July in Los Angeles is a sensory explosion—golden sunshine glinting off skyscrapers, the salt-tanged breeze of Venice Beach, and the electric hum of World Cup crowds. This month, the city transforms into a global stage, blending blockbuster events with intimate neighborhood gems. Whether you’re here for Matt Damon’s epic debut in "The Odyssey" at the TCL Chinese Theatre or a quiet sunset stroll through the Huntington’s lotus-filled gardens, L.A. in 2026 promises unforgettable moments.

July Highlights in Los Angeles

July 2026 is a month of celebration and competition in L.A. The FIFA World Cup brings matches to the Los Angeles Stadium (July 2) and quarterfinals (July 10), with fan zones at Venice Beach and Ovation Hollywood. The U.S. 250th Anniversary lights up the L.A. Memorial Coliseum with a star-spangled concert on July 4, while foodies flock to the Foodieland Festival at the Rose Bowl (July 16-17). Meanwhile, 108 free concerts pop up in parks citywide as part of Sounds of L.A. County, proving summer here is anything but quiet.

Best Neighborhoods to Explore

Arts District pulses with mural-splashed warehouses and craft breweries like Angel City, while Silver Lake offers hipster cafes and the Sunset Junction nightlife scene. For coastal charm, Venice Beach remains unrivaled—think street performers, skateboarders, and the newly revitalized boardwalk piers.

Cost of Living Snapshot

Budget for $2,250–$2,800/month for a central 1-bedroom apartment, or $1,700 in Koreatown. Dining out averages $18–$35 per meal, with splurges like Kato’s $165 tasting menu. A Metro pass costs $105/month, but ride-shares surge during World Cup events—opt for scooters along beach paths instead.

Things To Do in July

  • Cheer at World Cup fan zones (July 2–19) with giant screens and global cuisine
  • Paddle swan boats under fireworks at Echo Park Lake during the Lotus Festival (July 11–12)
  • Catch Christopher Nolan’s The Odyssey at outdoor screenings in Paramount Park
  • Surf alongside pros at the U.S. Open of Surfing in Huntington Beach (July 25–31)
  • Build Pokémon LEGO creations at the LEGO® Festival (July 20–Aug 16)
  • Stargaze at Mount Wilson Observatory’s Saturday night telescope sessions

Local Food Scene

At Lasita, savor Filipino roast chicken with calamansi glaze, or try Ètra’s squid-ink tagliatelle in a chic Arts District loft. For quick bites, Grand Central Market serves everything from Eggslut’s notorious sandwiches to maple-glazed donuts at The Donut Man. Don’t miss the Concern Foundation Block Party (July 19), where top chefs plate safari-themed dishes for charity.

Before You Go

Pack layers—coastal mornings are foggy (60s°F), while inland temps hit 90s°F. Book World Cup tickets and restaurant reservations 6+ weeks ahead. Traffic peaks July 1–5; use the Metro E Line to reach Santa Monica fireworks. Pro tip: Snag $34 same-day tickets to the Huntington via their app after 3pm.
